Frequently Asked Questions

How do hex nonogram clues work?

A standard nonogram has two axes of clues (rows and columns). In Gridsmith, the hexagonal grid creates three axes. Each line on each axis has a clue showing the run lengths of consecutive filled cells, just like a regular nonogram. Use all three axes together to deduce the solution — no guessing is ever required.

How do I fill and mark cells?

Select Fill or Mark at the bottom of the game screen, then tap a cell to apply that tool. Tap the same state again to clear it. Long-press a cell to use the alternate tool quickly without switching your selection.

What do the green clue numbers mean?

When a line's clue turns green, it means the filled cells in that line exactly match the expected clue pattern and the line is complete. This helps you track which lines are solved and which still need work.

Can I get a hint?

Yes — tap the Hint button (lightbulb icon) at the bottom of the game screen. It will correctly fill one cell that should be part of the solution. Hints are free and unlimited.

How do I restore my purchases?

Go to Settings → Restore Purchases. This will re-activate any puzzle packs or themes you've previously purchased on the same Apple ID.

My progress was lost. What happened?

Gridsmith saves progress locally on your device. If you uninstall and reinstall the app, or use the "Reset All Progress" option in Settings, your progress will be erased. Currently, Gridsmith does not sync progress across devices. We recommend keeping regular device backups via iCloud or iTunes.

Does Gridsmith work offline?

Yes, completely. Gridsmith requires no internet connection to play. All puzzles are generated and stored locally on your device.

Does Gridsmith collect any data?

No. Gridsmith collects no personal data, uses no analytics, and contains no ads or tracking. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

I found a bug or have a feature request.

Please email support@springdata.uk with a description of what happened, your device model, and iOS version. Screenshots are always helpful. We read every email and prioritise fixes based on player impact.
